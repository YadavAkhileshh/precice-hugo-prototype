<p class="lead mt-4">The Coupling Library for Partitioned Multi-Physics Simulations</p>
<p class="lead mt-2">
  preCICE is an <strong>open-source coupling library</strong> for partitioned multi-physics and multi-scale simulations,
  including fluid-structure interaction, conjugate heat transfer, and more.
</p>

{{< blocks/section color="dark" type="row" >}}

{{% blocks/feature icon="fas fa-puzzle-piece" title="Minimally Invasive" %}}
Elegant library approach with a high-level API in **C++, C, Fortran, Python, and Matlab**.
Couple your existing solvers with minimal code changes.
{{% /blocks/feature %}}

{{% blocks/feature icon="fas fa-expand-arrows-alt" title="Scalable" %}}
Pure **peer-to-peer approach** with support for heterogeneous hardware (CPU/GPU).
Proven on thousands of CPU cores — efficient also on a laptop.
{{% /blocks/feature %}}

{{% blocks/feature icon="fas fa-code-branch" title="Open Source" %}}
Free and open source under **LGPLv3**. Community-driven development at
TU Munich and University of Stuttgart. Everything developed openly on GitHub.
{{% /blocks/feature %}}

{{% blocks/feature icon="fas fa-flask" title="Battle-Tested" %}}
Used in dozens of academic and industrial projects worldwide — from
aerodynamics to biomechanics, wind energy, nuclear fusion, and more.
{{% /blocks/feature %}}

{{< /blocks/section >}}


{{< blocks/section color="white" type="row" >}}

{{% blocks/feature icon="fas fa-project-diagram" title="Arbitrary Solver Coupling" %}}
Arbitrary combinations of strong and weak interactions.
Couple **arbitrary many solvers** — no matter what language, grid, or time-stepping scheme.
{{% /blocks/feature %}}

{{% blocks/feature icon="fas fa-calculator" title="State-of-the-Art Numerics" %}}
Robust **quasi-Newton acceleration** and **radial-basis function data mapping**
for accurate and efficient multi-physics coupling.
{{% /blocks/feature %}}

{{% blocks/feature icon="fas fa-plug" title="Ready-to-Use Adapters" %}}
Official adapters for **OpenFOAM, CalculiX, FEniCS, deal.II, Nutils**, SU2, DUNE,
and more. Explore community projects for additional solvers.
{{% /blocks/feature %}}
